About me

I am someone who is endlessly curious about how things work — whether it’s a machine, an idea, or the universe itself. For me, engineering isn’t just a subject I study; it’s a way of looking at the world with questions in mind: Why does this work the way it does? What happens if I change it? How could it be better?

I’m fascinated by connections — between mechanical parts and living systems, between code and creativity, between the human mind and the cosmos. I enjoy experimenting, learning, and challenging myself, not because I want to check boxes of achievement, but because discovery feels natural to me.

At the same time, I value reflection as much as innovation. Philosophy helps me pause and think about meaning, while photography and time in nature remind me to notice the details that others might overlook. Whether I’m reading, playing chess, or cooking, I approach things with patience, curiosity, and a desire to grow.

What defines me most is not expertise, but the drive to keep exploring — to learn across disciplines, to connect ideas, and to see the world not just as it is, but as it could be.
